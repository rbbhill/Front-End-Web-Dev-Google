Kodiak Brown Bear

Did you know? Kodiak bears are the largest member of the bear family and the largest based land predator. The Kodiak bear can run very fast over a short distance and have been clocked at 40 mph (64 kph).

  • Scientific Name: Ursus arctos middendorffi
  • Average Lifespan: 28 years
  • Family Classification: Ursidae
  • Animal Class: Mammalia

Kodiak bears are a unique subspecies of the brown or grizzly bear. They live exclusively on the islands in the Kodiak Archipelago and have been isolated from other bears for about 12,000 years. There are about 3,500 Kodiak bears; a density of about 0.7 bears per square mile.
